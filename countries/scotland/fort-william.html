Getting Around

Walking
Fort William's High Street and waterfront are easily walkable. Town centre is compact — everything you need within 10 minutes on foot. Walkability is high in town.
Best for: High Street, restaurants, shops
ScotRail Train
The West Highland Line connects Fort William to Glasgow (3.5 hrs) — one of the most scenic train rides in the world. Also connects to Mallaig for ferries.
Best for: Arriving, Mallaig ferries
Car
Essential for reaching Glenfinnan, Glen Nevis, and the surrounding Highlands. Parking in town is straightforward. The A82 along Loch Lomond is stunning.
Best for: Day trips, trailheads, Glenfinnan
Getting to Skye
Take the train from Fort William to Mallaig (1.5 hrs), then the CalMac ferry to Armadale on Skye. A beautiful journey through some of Scotland's wildest coastline.

Landmarks & Must-See Spots

3 spots

Ben Nevis

Nature

The UK's highest peak at 1,345m. The Mountain Track (Pony Track) from Glen Nevis Visitor Centre is the most popular route — plan 7–9 hours round trip. Weather changes fast, even in summer.

Glenfinnan Viaduct

Iconic

The famous 21-arch railway viaduct from the Harry Potter films. Best viewed from the Glenfinnan Viaduct Viewpoint trail (10 min walk from car park). The Jacobite crosses around 10:45am and 3pm.

Glenfinnan, 25 min drive
Traveler recommended

Ben Nevis Visitor Centre

Nature

The starting point for the Mountain Track. Has a café, toilets, and parking (£4/day). Pick up a trail map and check conditions before heading up. Beautiful Glen Nevis surroundings.

Jacobite Steam Train

Ticketed

The famous "Harry Potter train" runs from Fort William to Mallaig over the Glenfinnan Viaduct. 84-mile round trip through some of Scotland's most dramatic scenery. Books up months in advance.

Ticketed

One of the oldest licensed distilleries in Scotland (est. 1825). Tours include a dram tasting — single malt whisky is naturally gluten-free. Located just north of town on the A82.

Travel Tips

  • Book the Jacobite Steam Train months in advance — it sells out fast, especially in summer. Morning departures are most popular.
  • Ask your hotel about GF packed lunches for hikes. Nevis Bank Inn does this — other hotels may too if you ask ahead.
  • Ben Nevis weather can be completely different from town. Check the Mountain Weather Information Service (MWIS) forecast before hiking, not just a regular weather app.
  • The Geographer doesn't take reservations in summer — go early (before 6pm) or expect to queue. Solo travelers get seated faster.
  • Fort William is the "Outdoor Capital of the UK" — great base for day trips to Glen Coe, Isle of Skye, and the Great Glen Way.
What I'd Do Differently
  • Actually watch for the Glenfinnan Viaduct from the train instead of missing it — sit on the left side heading to Mallaig!
  • Try Crannog at Garrison West for seafood — heard great things but ran out of time.
